Project & Demand Rationalisation — you don't need to be a manager to spot waste. If you see a project that has lost its purpose, this is how you flag it fast.

10–20%
Speed
Year 1
Impact
High
Do any of these sound familiar?

A project that has been "almost done" for 12 months. A deliverable nobody is asking for anymore. A workstream that quietly stopped having meetings but is still consuming budget. A request from last year that the business has moved on from.

If this sounds familiar, you may have a valuable cash preservation idea.

Here is exactly what to do. Right now.
1

Name the project or demand

Think about what your team is actively working on. Write down any project, initiative, or recurring request you personally question. A gut feeling counts at this stage.

Takes 5 minutes
2

Ask yourself one honest question

"If we stopped this today, would anyone notice in 30 days?" If the answer is no — or "probably not" — that is your idea.

No data needed
3

Estimate the cost — roughly is fine

How many people are on it? How many days a month? Multiply by a rough day rate. Example: 3 people × 10 days/month × QAR 2,000 = QAR 60K/month.

Rough numbers are fine
4

Share the idea in a clear sentence

Write it in one clear sentence: what the project is, what it costs roughly, and why you think it can be paused or stopped. You do not need to prove it. Capture it clearly and share it for review.

Named submission
What a good submission looks like
✅ Actionable — aim for this

"The weekly automated report sent to 3 executives has not been opened in 4 months. It runs on a QAR 8K/month server job. Suggest we cancel it."

❌ Too vague to act on

"We should review our projects and cut unnecessary ones."

Your idea could unlock real savings.

You do not need to have every detail. A clear observation and a practical note are enough to start the conversation.

around a broken or

Technology Debt Reduction — every workaround, every "only Ahmed knows how this works," every "we export to Excel because the system can't do it" is costing money daily.

Savings range
10–40%
Timeline
Strategic
Impact
High
Tech debt is like a leak in the roof you have stopped noticing.

You adapt to it. You put a bucket under it. You tell new joiners "just do not touch that." Meanwhile it quietly damages everything around it — and the longer you leave it, the more expensive the fix becomes. The first step to fixing it is admitting it exists.

Tick what you recognise in your area.
✓
A system that only one or two people know how to operate
If that person leaves, we are in serious trouble. Key-person dependency is a form of tech debt with a real cost.
✓
A system running on software or hardware past its end-of-life
Unsupported software costs premium extended support fees and creates security risks requiring expensive compensating controls.
✓
A manual workaround that exists because a system does not work properly
"We export to Excel and reformat it because the system cannot talk to the other system." That Excel process is paid human time covering a system failure.
✓
A system we are migrating away from — but still paying full licence costs on
Classic tech debt: paying for the old and the new simultaneously during a prolonged migration.
✓
A custom-built internal tool that nobody maintains properly anymore
Built years ago by someone who left. No documentation. Any change requires expensive one-off development work.
How to flag tech debt as a cash preservation idea.
1

Describe the system and the debt

What is the system? How old is it? What is broken, outdated, or unsupported? What workarounds exist because of it?

Be specific and honest
2

Estimate what the debt is costing

Support contract cost, hours spent on workarounds per week, risk of failure or breach. Even a rough estimate makes the idea powerful.

Rough numbers are fine
3

Suggest a direction and share it with the ERP, S/4, or Data leadership team

Does S/4 HANA cover this? Could it be migrated to cloud? You do not need the full answer — just point the way.

Point the way; you do not need to solve it alone

Use Copilot to size the problem

Open Copilot and describe the legacy system:

📋 Copy this prompt

The most expensive tech debt is the kind nobody admits to.

You have been working around it. Now is the time to name it out loud. That is the first — and hardest — step to fixing it.
